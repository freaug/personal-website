About Me

Eddie Farr attended Georgia Southern University, where he earned a BA in Music (saxophone) and an MM in Technology.

As an undergraduate student, he held principal positions in the university's top wind ensemble and jazz band. As a member of the jazz band he had the privilege of sharing the stage with noted jazz trombonist Harry Watters and legendary trumpeter John Faddis. He also performed at the 2012 U.S. Navy International Saxophone Symposium with fellow saxophonist Alex Sellers and at the 2011 North American Saxophone Alliance Region 6 Conference with the Georgia Southern Saxophone Quartet.

Eddie first became interested in music technology after performing Buku by JacobTV at Georgia Southern University's electronic music concert series Channel Noise.

In graduate school, he composed several pieces, focusing on live sound processing and the spatialization of sound. He used ambisonics in his compositions and has explored other methods of sound spatialization, including high order ambisonics and vector-based amplitude panning. Eddie also worked with Focusrites RedNet 2, an audio networking interface.

Along with live sound processing, Eddie became interested in live visual processing to accompany his compositions. He worked significantly with the Max Jitter package, as well as the Pure Data GEM external, creating human and audio controlled interfaces for visual generation and manipulation.
